Created in 1885, before Arizona was recognized as a state, the University of Arizona was born as a borderless institution, geographically positioned in the heart of the Southwest for boundless impact in the region, the world, and outer space. In 2016 alone, the UA formalized academic and research institutional partnership agreements with over 35 international universities.

We are the collaborators.

Disruptive Change in Higher Education
To increase the global impact of the University of Arizona (UA), the Office of International Education at the University has partnered with universities across the world to designate off-site UA spaces on foreign campuses. Beginning in 2017, these micro-campuses enable the international delivery of the same quality on-site and online UA degree programs to students abroad, eliminating the traditional barriers of space and time to target education equity disparities around the globe. As a synergistic hub for other forms of internationalization, including faculty research collaborations, student intercultural learning and development, and interdisciplinary community engagement, a micro-campus offers students at partner universities the same first-tier opportunities characteristic of the Wildcat experience. For Fall 2017, the University of Arizona announced the following eight locations for micro-campus study: UA Phnom Penh, UA Qingdao, UA Amman, UA Hanoi, UA Puebla, UA Shenzhen, UA Sharjah, and UA Manila.
Expanding Student Exchange in the Americas
In October 2016, the UA received a 100,000 Strong in the Americas Innovation Fund grant through the joint White House, Department of State, and Coca-Cola initiative to expand higher education partnerships in water sustainability in the Americas. The award funds 10 graduate students at the UA and its partner universities, who are enrolled in dual graduate study in two disciplines - environmental sciences and engineering, enabling students to spend one year at their home institution and the second at the partner university. Receipt of this award propelled UA to become the most frequent recipient of the 100,000 Strong in the Americas Innovation Fund since the grant’s inception in 2014.
FlexTech Alliance for a Manufacturing Innovation Institute
In 2015, the DoD awarded one of the UA’s partnership organizations, FlexTech Alliance, a cooperative agreement to develop a Manufacturing Innovation Institute for Flexible Hybrid Electronics. Building upon the interdisciplinary research efforts of UA health sciences, engineering, and optical sciences faculty to advance wearable sensor systems and applications, the five-year, $75 million DoD award is being matched by more than $96 million in cost sharing from non-federal sources, including private companies, universities, several U.S. states, the city of San Jose, CA, and not-for-profit organizations. The institute is part of the National Network for Manufacturing Innovation program, an executive branch initiative to support advanced manufacturing in the U.S.
